## What Does the Ethereum Layer‑2 Surge Mean for Tokenization?

The Layer‑2 surge drives **record tokenization activity**, boosting liquidity and market participation. It also forces **regulatory frameworks** to adapt to faster digital asset issuance.

## Why does this matter for the market?
Ethereum's gas fees dropped 35% to $1.20 per transaction, while the total value locked (TVL) on Layer‑2 networks reached $25 billion, a 12% weekly gain. This cost reduction fuels broader dApp adoption and encourages DeFi projects to innovate, solidifying Ethereum's lead in tokenized asset pipelines.

## What are the future implications?
In the next 6‑12 months, Layer‑2 platforms are projected to handle over 40% of new token issuances, spurring cross‑chain interoperability standards and prompting regulators to craft specific guidance for account‑abstracted assets.
